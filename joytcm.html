About

Joy Tan-Chi Mendoza is a Filipino author, speaker, and advocate known for her inspiring journey of healing and forgiveness following a traumatic experience in her youth. Born to Dr. Peter Tan-Chi, founder and senior pastor of Christ's Commission Fellowship (CCF) in the Philippines, and his wife Deonna, Joy was raised in a devout Christian family alongside her three siblings.

At the age of 15, Joy endured a harrowing ordeal when she and her friends were subjected to a robbery and multiple assaults in their home. Despite the severity of the situation, Joy found solace in her faith, experiencing a profound sense of peace amidst the chaos. This inner calm allowed her to later forgive her assailants, a decision rooted in her belief that God's love transcends human understanding.

Choosing forgiveness over bitterness, Joy embarked on a path of healing, supported by her family and faith community. She later shared her story in her book, "When A Good God Allows Rape," published in 2015. The book offers insights into her personal journey and serves as a beacon of hope for others facing similar traumas.

Joy is married to Edric Mendoza, a prominent television host and anchor. Together, they have five children, whom Joy homeschools. Beyond her role as a mother, she actively engages in speaking engagements across the Philippines, focusing on marriage, parenting, and faith-based topics. Her ministry work includes counseling women who have experienced sexual abuse, providing them with support and guidance rooted in Christian teachings.

Through her writings and public engagements, Joy continues to inspire many, demonstrating that with faith and forgiveness, it is possible to overcome profound adversity and find purpose beyond pain:
