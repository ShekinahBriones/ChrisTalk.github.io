Casting Cronws

Worship & Music

About

Casting Crowns has always been known for distilling life’s challenges into powerful proclamations of faith, offering singable truths that point to Jesus. Their latest album, Healer, stays true to this mission, delivering a collection of songs that reflect on life’s struggles while emphasizing the ultimate source of healing—Christ.

Casting Crowns is a contemporary Christian and Christian rock band that originated in Daytona Beach, Florida in 1999, founded by youth pastor and lead vocalist Mark Hall. What began as a local student ministry band quickly blossomed into one of the most successful and influential groups in Christian music history.

Initially formed as a worship band at First Baptist Church in Daytona Beach, the original lineup included Mark Hall, guitarists Juan DeVevo and Hector Cervantes, violinist Melodee DeVevo, and drummer Rob Cervantes. In 2001, the band relocated to McDonough, Georgia, where new members joined: Chris Huffman (bass), Megan Garrett (keys, accordion), and Andy Williams (drums), further shaping their distinctive sound.

Their self-titled debut album Casting Crowns (2003) marked a turning point. The single "Voice of Truth" dominated Christian radio, holding the 1 spot for 14 weeks and later becoming part of the the Giants film soundtrack. The album went platinum by 2005, with their track "Who Am I" also achieving gold certification.
