Jesus Culture

Worship & Music

About

Jesus Culture began in 1999 as a youth movement with a mission to ignite “revival in our day.” What started as a local gathering soon expanded into a global phenomenon, shaping modern worship and inspiring believers worldwide. Through worship, events, training, and resources, Jesus Culture continues to equip the church and encourage a deeper connection with God’s presence.

A significant turning point for Jesus Culture was the release of their album Everything in 2005, which was recorded live and introduced a fresh worship experience. This album resonated with people globally and marked the beginning of a new era in worship music. Over the years, Jesus Culture has released 22 albums, producing powerful anthems such as How He Loves, Your Love Never Fails, Set A Fire, Break Every Chain, Spirit Break Out, and Holy Spirit. These songs have not only shaped contemporary Christian music but have also become integral parts of worship services in churches worldwide.

Beyond music, Jesus Culture has fostered a culture of revival that extends to discipleship and leadership training. Their conferences and events draw thousands of worshippers, creating an environment where believers can encounter God’s presence in a transformative way. Through these initiatives, Jesus Culture seeks to inspire a new generation to God wholeheartedly and spread His love through worship and service.

In recent years, a new wave of worship leaders has emerged from Jesus Culture’s local churches, carrying forward the original vision of revival. These leaders are writing new songs that continue to impact the church and lead people into deeper worship. This ongoing passion and dedication fuel Jesus Culture’s mission to awaken believers to God’s presence and power in their lives.

Ultimately, Jesus Culture is more than just a worship band—it is a movement that has redefined modern worship and countless believers to seek God with renewed passion. As they continue to create music, train leaders, and cultivate a culture of revival, their impact on the global church remains profound and enduring.
